Introduction

Children are born ready to learn and understand the world around them. It is natural for them to use all their abilities to learn. From birth, children are learning about themselves, other people and the world around them, playing an active role in their own learning and development.

The first 2 to 6 years of a child’s life are globally acknowledged to be the most critical years for lifelong development, since the pace of development in these years is extremely rapid. Recent research in the field of neuroscience, provided convincing evidence of the ‘critical periods’ located within these early years for the forming of synaptic connections in the brain and for the full development of the brain’s potential. Research has also indicated that if these early years are not supported by or embedded in, a stimulating and enriching physical and psychosocial environment, the chances of the child’s brain developing to its full potential are considerably, often irreversible and reduced.

This stage in life is also important as a foundation for the inculcation of social values and personal habits, which are known to last a lifetime.

What follows logically is the crucial importance of investing in these early years to ensure an enabling environment for every child and thereby a sound foundation for life, which is not only the right of every child but which will also impact in the long term the quality of human capital available to a country.

Making the most of childhood - the importance of the early years

Learning Centers

As an integral part of the Edu-Paedia Integrated Curriculum for Early Years, we provide two types of Integrated learning environment - Classrooms with Enrichment Learning Centers and Classrooms with Exploratory Learning Centers.

Enrichment learning center

Enrichment Centers are designed to offer students a variety of learning alternatives as an addition to a common unit of instruction. These centers are typically designed outside the classroom in a separate enclosure and students visit these centers twice or thrice a week. These centers are used after the presentation of important materials or concepts. These are designed to provide students with opportunities to enrich and enhance their appreciation and understanding of the topics through individual/ pair/ small group experiences in the center.

Exploratory Learning centers

Exploratory learning centers are often called learning zones or stations, provide a variety of curriculum activities using cognitive modes of learning, such as visual, auditory, and kinesthetic inside the classroom. They offer a wonderful opportunity for facilitators to actively engage children in meaningful, hands-on experiences which are pursued at child's own pace and level of curiosity.

After introductory instruction of a particular concept, the facilitator introduces/assigns students to various exploratory learning centers to explore the information presented or as an extend learning in that particular concept. Or at times, students are engaged in their own selection of activities during free time, upon arrival in the morning or just prior to dismissal as a “free-choice” activity during the day. These centers allow students to engage in meaningful discoveries that match their individual interests.

Well-planned learning stations present children with challenging projects and opportunities for exploration in day to day exploration of concepts. They encourage thinking skills and motor development which are the crucial patterns for development at this age band.

Learning centers are not utopian. They are solidly founded on the theory of constructivism and multiple intelligences. They tap and build on the natural curiosity of the child, playful exploration making quick connections and learning holistically.

Role of Teacher

A positive caring relationship that is ongoing – This is the most important factor in supporting a child’s learning.

Adults who appreciate the uniqueness of each child, who respect and respond to the child’s feelings, needs and interests.

Materials & experiences to learn from, time to get involved with them. Young children need chances to get actively involved. ‘Hands-on, minds-on’ is the expression used.

Opportunities to ‘be in the world doing things’. Children need to be actively involved in meaningful experiences. Learning happens best in context when learning experiences are interwoven to the real world experiences.

A group experience - Time to really get involved and build relationships with other children and adults both socially and emotionally.

Through careful observation we can assure that individual developmental needs are being met. The Program is a flexible, yet structured program designed to enable your child to explore and grow in their ever changing world. The program consists of short, structured activities where your child explores and learns while improving their fine motor skills, gross motor skills, and social skills. The Program is age-appropriate, geared to the individual needs of each child and offered in a warm and caring environment.

The children are involved with a specific set of teachers and can enjoy a variety of activities in their company. We make sure that we maintain program flexibility because these early years mean rapidly changing needs as each child grows through many stages of development. Our curriculum addresses all areas of development. We do this by carefully planning and coordinating five areas:

  • EQ (Emotional Intelligence).

  • Language & Literacy.

  • Science & Sensory.

  • Fine & Gross Motor Skills.

  • Creative Expression.

The Kindergarten curriculum is divided into four areas: Language & Literacy~ Creative Express~ Science & Sensory Gross Motor Skills. The fifth area Emotional Intelligence/Social Studies is taught whenever the opportunity arises and also specifically and deliberately in all of the other curriculum areas. Each curriculum area offers a different style of learning, appropriate materials and equipment, alternates between child-initiated and teacher-directed activities. They are all integrated with the thematic approach and coordinated lesson planning.

The four curriculum areas are taught by teacher(s), in classrooms specifically designed and equipped for each subject. The children are in a cohesive group, enjoying relationships with one another for the school year and have the advantages of utilizing specialized classroom as well as the outdoor play.

At 'Edu-Paedia' our goal is to create an environment where there is respect for age, gender and cultural differences. Themes are introduced every month with weekly sub themes to help the children discover the similarities as well as the differences among ourselves and others.
